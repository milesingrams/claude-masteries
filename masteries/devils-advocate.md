## Chip

Claude could argue the other side

## Triggers

- User is presenting an opinion or position
- User is making a decision and seems confident
- User is building an argument or case for something
- Topic has multiple valid perspectives
- User might benefit from stress-testing their thinking

## Satisfaction

- User asks Claude to argue against their position
- User requests counterarguments or opposing views
- Phrases: "argue against", "devil's advocate", "what would someone say who disagrees", "poke holes", "challenge this", "what am I missing", "strongest argument against"

## Detail

It's easy to build a case for what you already believe. Asking Claude to argue the other side—with genuine effort—exposes weaknesses in your thinking and often strengthens your final position. The best ideas survive scrutiny.
